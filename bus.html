Hopeful Coder Has Varied, Globetrotting Work History

I originally attended college to go to nursing school as a way to pay to become a physical therapist. That profession caught my eye towards the end of my high school years when I was stuck going to physical therapy to help me recover from back-to-back ACL replacements.

Unfortuantely that plan did not last very long. I soon learned after my first week of classes that I was already a year behind due to not being registered in the correct classes (something got lost in translation between my freshman advisor). That year I wasn't sure what I was going to do.

I ended up spending that summer in Texas with family and went to a lot of Texas Rangers baseball games. I read a now defunct national sports page called The National and fell in love with the idea of a being a sports journalist. I switched majors to journalism soon after.

Ultimately I earned a journalism degree from the University of Texas at Arlington. I was working at The Dallas Morning News' Sports Day at the time, not as an active journalist out in the field but rather as an agate editor. Instead of focusing on one sport I was able to dip my toes in sports and leagues across the globe.

That was a bucket list-like job, working for my hometown paper and helping cover some of my favorite teams, then 9/11 happened. I felt I needed to see the world and volunteered to be an English as a Foreign Language teacher, thus I began my journey in education at the Center for Foreign Languages in the Central Asian town of Djizzakh, Uzbekistan.

Upon my return I worked in education, mainly within a Special Education environment, for the next few years. After working in the classroom I spent time working in call centers, where I eally enjoyed my time working for a client in the financial sector.

Lately I tried my hand a selling life insurance. That experience confirmed that I am not a salesman, even though I come from a line of salesmen. I'm excited for the opportunity to learn a skill that I can apply for the rest of my life.
