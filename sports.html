Classic Hangman

From Various Boards to Books and Bikes with Everything Inbetween

My life outside of the work environment is fairly typical. I spend time with family and friends, enjoying riding around my community and am an avid gamer (be it backyard, board, card, or video, I do no discriminate). I also tend to spend an inordinate amount of time reading everthing from the local newspaper to global websites.

Like most kids my bike was my main mode of transportation growing up. Most of my youth was spent in small rural towns so it afforded me the opportunity to enjoy long, uninterrupted rides. So my love of bike riding was planted long ago, which is good since it's the best form of exercise for my catastrophically bad knees.

After a particularly bad winter for my knees I bought a new bike. It quickly went from a form of recreation to my preferred option for travel. Within a few months I was riding over 300 miles a month and pedaled just shy of 4,000 miles the first year. I now spend more on my bike accessories than I do car accessories, I can safely say my bike has more smart technology than my car.

Another way I like to spend my free time is by gaming, whether it be the latest AAA title to hit the shelves (Borderlands 3 will be my next obsession) or a perfectly executed card-driven board game I'm in. The game I've played the most the past few years has been Twilight Struggle, a board game that recreates the Cold War (it is a cross between Risk and chess, with a healthy side of paranoia).

It is a tense experience that is tough to beat, in fact a friend and I have played the game well over 500 times. We regularly find ourselves talking about the different situations that took place in the last game (or earlier in the current game), the game has a way of pulling you into the theme as you try to tease out a bit of info to use next time.

Another hobby of mine is being a sound engineer for some local musicains. I've run the board at a few shows over the years and have produced several music videos for friends of the band. I can also be found out on the links, although not nearly as often as I would like (the lament of every golfer).
